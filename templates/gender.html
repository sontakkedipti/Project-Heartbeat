Heart Disease by Gender

We've looked at the differences in heart disease between men and women. As the count of underlying disceases increases, chances of getting heart discease increases proportionally. This is also known as Comorbidity - The Presence of more than one medical condition in a patient that may contribute to clinical progression of the primary disorder. We've taken the data to analyze how comorbidity affects men and women with heart disease and we've noticed that men have a slightly higher chance of getting heart disease than women
